Resolution of Marriage via Mutual Consent (Sec 13B)

A comprehensive study of H.M.P. No. 43/2023: Navigating the complexities of legal separation with dignity and professional precision.

CourtCivil Judge Sr. Div, Saoner
01. Background

Understanding the Irretrievable Breakdown

In this matter, the petitioners (husband and wife) approached our firm after a brief period of marital cohabitation. The marriage, solemnized in July 2021, faced significant challenges early on due to deep-seated differences in perspective and lifestyle expectations. Within weeks, the parties recognized that the union was no longer sustainable.

Separation as a Legal Prerequisite: Under Indian Law, specifically the Hindu Marriage Act, a couple must be living separately for at least one continuous year before they can file for divorce by mutual consent. In this case, our firm meticulously documented the separation period starting from July 26, 2021, to ensure the petition met all statutory thresholds.

02. Legal Literacy

What is Section 13(B) Divorce?

For our clients and visitors, it is essential to understand that "Mutual Consent" is the most peaceful way to resolve a marriage that is no longer working. Here are the core pillars:

Mutual Agreement

Both parties must agree that the marriage should end. Neither party is "blamed" or accused of cruelty, making the process significantly less stressful.

Asset Settlement

All matters regarding Streedhan (gifts/jewelry), alimony, and joint assets are settled privately before entering the courtroom.

Waiting Period

Usually, there is a 6-month "cooling off" period, but in cases of extreme separation, the Court can "waive" this to speed up the process.

Strategic Advantage in this Case

In H.M.P. 43/2023, ADV. Ashish V. Linpane successfully moved a plea to waive the 6-month statutory waiting period. Because the parties had already lived apart for nearly two years, the Court recognized that further delay would only cause mental agony. This reduced the case duration to just 4 months.

03. The Journey

How We Handled the Litigation

Step 1

Counseling & Mediation

Before filing, we ensure both parties are making a voluntary choice. We facilitate a "Settlement Deed" where all belongings are exchanged, preventing future disputes.

Step 2

Filing the Joint Petition

The petition (Exhibit 1) is filed. Both parties appear before the Hon'ble Judge to confirm their intent. This is known as the "First Motion."

Step 3

Court Verification

The Court records the statements of both individuals. The judge verifies that there is no "coercion" (forcing someone to sign) or fraud involved.

Step 4

Final Decree

Upon satisfaction, the court passes a Judgment and Decree of Divorce. The marriage is legally dissolved, and both parties are free to start their new chapters.

04. Outcome

Closing the Chapter with Dignity

On July 21, 2023, the Senior Division Court at Saoner delivered its final verdict. The Hon'ble Judge S.A. Sardar observed that the "consent of none of the parties is obtained by force, fraud, or coercion."
