Dissolution of Marriage Under Section 13(B)

Petition No.H.M.P. 43/2023
Counsel in ChargeADV. Ashish V. Linpane
JurisdictionSenior Division, Saoner
StatusMarriage Dissolved

I. The Marital Background & Conflict

The Petitioner No. 1 (Husband) and Petitioner No. 2 (Wife) solemnized their marriage on 09.07.2021 as per Hindu Rites and Rituals. However, shortly after the union, irreconcilable differences in opinion and temperament emerged, rendering the marital bond unsustainable. The record indicates that the parties began residing separately as of July 2021, marking a definitive cessation of the marital cohabitation.

During the separation period, which exceeded the statutory requirement of one year, the parties reached a comprehensive settlement regarding all ancillary matters, including the exchange of gifts, ornaments, and home appliances. Under the professional guidance of ADV. Ashish V. Linpane, a petition was moved for a decree of divorce by mutual consent, prioritizing an amicable and dignified resolution over prolonged litigation.

Legal Framework

The petition was filed under Section 13(B) of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955. This provision allows for the dissolution of marriage when both parties mutually agree that the marriage has broken down irretrievably and they have lived separately for a period of at least one year.

II. Points for Determination & Judicial Findings

The Honorable Court conducted a thorough inquiry into the voluntariness of the consent and the possibility of reconciliation. The findings of the court, supported by the evidence presented by our firm, were as follows:

Statutory Requirement Judicial Finding
Has the statutory period of separation been fulfilled? Yes. The court verified the separation since 26.07.2021, exceeding the required duration.
Is there any scope for reconciliation? Negative. Multiple reconciliation efforts failed; the court observed no likelihood of reunion.
Was consent obtained via fraud or coercion? No. The court found that both parties moved the petition freely and with full understanding of the consequences.

III. Final Decree and Annulment

Following the waiver of the traditional six-month cooling-off period (given the long-term separation), the Court expressed satisfaction with the averments in the petition. The judgment delivered on 21.07.2023 officially dissolved the marriage between the petitioners.

This case highlights the efficacy of Section 13(B) in providing a streamlined exit for parties who recognize the end of their marital journey. ADV. Ashish V. Linpane ensured that all respective obligations were met, allowing both individuals to move forward with legal finality and peace of mind.
