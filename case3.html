The Story Behind the Case

Imagine a child being raised in a loving home, but every time they go to school or apply for a document, their paperwork shows a different father's name and a different surname. This was the reality for the plaintiffs, who had legally adopted a son named Ruhan.

Although the adoption was officially registered, the Municipal Council (Birth Records) refused to change the child's birth certificate. They argued that a birth certificate is a historical record of "who gave birth" and cannot be edited just because a child is adopted later in life.

Why is this difficult to resolve?

In India, birth registers are considered permanent historical logs. The Registrar can fix a "clerical error" (like a typo), but they cannot change parentage or surnames based on adoption without a specific order from a Judge. This creates a "deadlock" for parents who just want their child's papers to match their reality.

Our Strategy & Work

Adv. Sandhya V. Linpane and Adv. Akshay V. Linpane teamed up to tackle this challenge. Our focus was on the child's "Right to Identity." We presented the Registered Adoption Deed as the primary evidence of the new family bond.

We argued that forcing a child to carry two different names—one in school and one on a birth certificate—would cause massive confusion in the future, affecting everything from bank accounts to marriage certificates and passports.

The Court's Final Decision

On June 21, 2024, the Court delivered a judgment that protected the child's future. The Judge declared that the child's name is now officially "Ruhan Sachin Nagrale."

What this means for the family: While the law keeps the original birth record for historical purposes, this Court Declaration now acts as the master legal document. Whenever the family needs to prove Ruhan's identity, they show this Judgment. It legally overrides the old name and confirms his new parentage to every government department.

The family can now live with peace of mind, knowing that Ruhan’s official identity is finally unified with his home life.
